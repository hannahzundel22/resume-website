In the Kitchen

The Art of Pie

There's something almost meditative about making a pie from scratch — the cold butter worked into flour, the way a lattice top comes together, and the smell that fills the whole house while it bakes. Pie is my love language.

Nothing beats a homemade lattice pie fresh from the oven.

Why I Love Pie

Every pie is a little different. The crust changes with the weather, the filling changes with the season, and there's always something new to learn. It's endlessly creative and deeply comforting at the same time.

My Pie Philosophy

Always use cold butter. Never skip the blind bake on custard pies. And share every slice — pie tastes better when someone else is smiling across the table from you.

My Pie-Making Process

  1. Gather your ingredients
    • All-purpose flour, salt, sugar
    • Very cold unsalted butter
    • Ice water (a few tablespoons)
    • Your favorite seasonal filling
  2. Make the crust
    • Cut butter into flour until pea-sized
    • Add ice water slowly until dough holds together
    • Chill for at least 30 minutes — don't skip this!
  3. Prepare the filling
    • Fresh fruit in summer — peaches, berries, cherries
    • Apples and pears in fall
    • Classic pumpkin or pecan for holidays
  4. Bake and enjoy
    • Brush crust with egg wash for golden color
    • Bake until bubbling and beautifully browned
    • Let it cool (the hardest part)

On the Trail

Life is Better Outside

Hiking is my reset button. Whether it's a quick morning trail or an all-day summit push, being outside in the fresh air with nothing but the sound of wind and footsteps is the best kind of quiet. Utah and the Pacific Northwest are two of the most beautiful places I've been lucky enough to explore on foot.

There's no view you haven't earned on a hike.

Favorite Terrain

Mountain trails with a payoff view at the top. I love the mix of forest shade and open ridgelines — especially when wildflowers are in bloom in early summer.

What I Bring

Water, snacks (obviously), sunscreen, and a camera. The best hikes always end with a photo of the view and a long drive home with great music.
